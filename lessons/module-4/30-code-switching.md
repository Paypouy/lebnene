# Lesson 30 — How Lebanese is actually spoken

> The famous greeting "hi, kifak, ça va?" is a documentary fact. This
> lesson teaches him to stop being thrown by it.

## The mixing rules (descriptive)
- French owns: politeness (bonjour, merci, pardon), numbers in shops, food terms
- English owns: work/tech (meeting, deadline, ok, sorry), youth slang
- The matrix language stays Arabic: "bookayt el meeting la bukra" — English noun, Arabic grammar wrapped around it. He can do this with his German work brain immediately: the verb morphology is the skill.

## Listening lab (the heart of this lesson)
Three 60–90s native clips (teacher curates: a podcast snippet, a vox-pop
street interview, a cooking video):
1. First pass: count code-switches.
2. Second pass: gist in one sentence.
3. Third pass with transcript: mark every reduced form heard vs. textbook form.

## Drills
1. Reduction dictation: teacher speaks reduced, student writes the FULL form.
2. Shadowing: play 10s of native audio, student repeats imitating rhythm exactly (not understanding — imitating). 5 rounds.
3. Code-switch production: describe your workday mixing naturally: 3andé meeting l-sé3a 3ashra, ba3dén deadline…
