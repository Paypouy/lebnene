# Lesson 28 — Storytelling

## Grammar point: the narrative toolkit
- **fa** = so/then (glue between events)
- **2ém + verb** = "so then he up and…" (story-only, very Lebanese)
- **kén 3am + verb** = was doing (background): kenet 3am bemshé… (I was walking…)
- Dramatic present: switch INTO present at the climax — w fij2a bshuf… (suddenly I see…)

## Model story (teacher performs, student deconstructs)

| Arabic | Translit | English |
|---|---|---|
| مرة كنت عم بستنى سرفيس بالأشرفية… | marra kenet 3am bestanna serviis bil-Ashrafiyyé… | Once I was waiting for a serviis in Achrafieh… |
| فا وقف واحد، طلعت، | fa wa22af wé7ad, tle3et, | So one stopped, I got in, |
| وبعد خمس دقايق بيقلي: إنت مش من هون، صح؟ | w ba3ed khams da2éye2 by2ellé: inta mish min hon, sa77? | and after five minutes he goes: you're not from here, right? |
| قلتلو: كيف عرفت؟ | 2eltello: kif 3refet? | I said: how'd you know? |
| قام قلّي: حطيت حزام! | 2ém 2allé: 7attét 7zém! | So he goes: you put on a seatbelt! |

## Drills
1. Connector insertion: bare event list → student welds it with fa/ba3dén/2ém/fij2a.
2. Background vs. event: kén 3am… lamma… (was doing X when Y) — 6 builds.
3. Punchline timing: same story told flat vs. with dramatic-present climax.

## Role-play variations
- Student tells a REAL story from his life in Lebanon (there's always a serviis story by now).
- Story tennis: alternate sentences to co-build an absurd story.
