# Lesson 26 — WhatsApp Lebanese

> Lebanese friends will text him in arabizi (the 3/7/2 Latin system) more
> than in Arabic script. He already knows it from our vocab tables — now
> it becomes a reading skill at speed.

## Sample chat (read aloud, fast)

```
Sami:  ki fak? wenak mn zaman!
Daniel: hahaha sorry kenet maghru2 bil sheghel
Sami:  tayyeb el sabt 3azimé 3nde, jeyé?
Daniel: akid jéyé! 2addé el sé3a?
Sami:  8, bas ta3a 7:30 sa3edné shwayy 😅
Daniel: tamém jéyé 7:30, shu jib ma3é?
Sami:  wala shi bas 7alak ✌️
Daniel: ya3ne jéyé bi knéfé. khalas.
```

## Voice-note etiquette
- Lebanese voice notes start mid-thought: "é shu, mar7aba, esma3né…"
- Closers: yalla bye / yalla mnshufak / ok 7abibé bye
- It is 100% normal to send 4 voice notes in a row. He should too.

## Drills
1. Arabizi sprint: 10 chat lines read aloud against the clock, then translated.
2. Script↔arabizi conversion both directions (5 sentences each).
3. Voice-note structure: opener + 2 points + closer, 30s, three takes.

## Role-play variations
- Live chat: teacher and student text in arabizi for 5 minutes, real phones.
- The 4-voice-note chain: tell one story across 4 short notes, naturally split.
