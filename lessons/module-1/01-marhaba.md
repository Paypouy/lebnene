# Lesson 01 — Mar7aba! Greetings & introductions

**Scenario:** First meeting with a Lebanese colleague. You can greet, give
your name, ask theirs, say how you are, and close politely.

## Warm-up (5 min)
First lesson: teacher greets the student in Arabic repeatedly with different
greetings; student answers with whatever they can. Sets the rule: **the
lesson happens in Arabic.**

## Dialogue

> Sami meets Daniel, the new colleague, at the office kitchen.

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sami | مرحبا! | mar7aba! | Hallo! | Hello! |
| Daniel | مرحبتين! | mar7abtén! | Hallo zurück! (wörtl. „zwei Hallos") | Hello back! (lit. "two hellos") |
| Sami | كيفك؟ | kifak? | Wie geht's dir? (m) | How are you? (m) |
| Daniel | منيح، الحمدلله. وإنت كيفك؟ | mni7, l7amdella. w inta kifak? | Gut, Gott sei Dank. Und dir? | Good, thank God. And you? |
| Sami | تمام. شو اسمك؟ | tamém. shu esmak? | Bestens. Wie heißt du? | Great. What's your name? |
| Daniel | أنا اسمي دانيال. وإنت؟ | ana esmé Daniel. w inta? | Ich heiße Daniel. Und du? | My name is Daniel. And you? |
| Sami | أنا سامي. أهلا وسهلا فيك! | ana Sami. ahla w sahla fik! | Ich bin Sami. Herzlich willkommen! | I'm Sami. Welcome! |
| Daniel | تشرّفنا! | tsharrafna! | Sehr erfreut! | Pleased to meet you! |
| Sami | يلّا، منشوفك! | yalla, menshufak! | Also dann, man sieht sich! | Alright, see you around! |
| Daniel | بخاطرك! | bkhatrak! | Tschüss! (vom Gehenden gesagt) | Goodbye! (said by the one leaving) |

## Grammar point: pronouns + the -ak/-ik switch

| | Pronoun | "your name" | "how are you" |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | أنا ana | esmé (my name) | — |
| you (m) | إنت inta | esmak | kifak |
| you (f) | إنتي inté | esmik | kifik |

Everything addressed to a woman flips **-ak → -ik**. Drill it now, forever.

## Drills (15–20 min)
1. **Substitution:** teacher says a name → student: "ana esmé ___, w inta shu esmak?"
2. **Gender flip:** teacher says kifak? → student answers AND re-asks as kifik? (alternate)
3. **Rapid Q&A:** mar7aba → student must produce mar7abtén instantly; kifak → mni7 l7amdella, w inta? Ten rounds, increasing speed.

## Role-play variations (20+ min)
- Meet a colleague (formal-ish): add تشرفنا.
- Meet a friend's friend at a café (relaxed): yalla + first names only.
- Complication: the other person answers مش منيح "mish mni7" (not good) — student must react: شو في؟ "shu fi?" (what's wrong?) — teach on the spot.
