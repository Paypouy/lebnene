# Lesson 04 — Where are you from?

**Scenario:** The "new foreigner" conversation, mastered. You answer the
five inevitable questions smoothly and ask them back.

## Warm-up
Numbers blitz (L03), greetings chain.

## Dialogue

> A taxi driver interrogates Daniel, as taxi drivers do.

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Driver | من وين إنت؟ | min wén inta? | Woher kommst du? | Where are you from? |
| Daniel | أنا ألماني، من برلين. | ana almané, min Berlin. | Ich bin Deutscher, aus Berlin. | I'm German, from Berlin. |
| Driver | أهلا وسهلا! شو عم تعمل بلبنان؟ | ahla w sahla! shu 3am ta3mel b-lebnén? | Willkommen! Was machst du im Libanon? | Welcome! What are you doing in Lebanon? |
| Daniel | عم اشتغل هون. | 3am eshteghel hon. | Ich arbeite hier. | I'm working here. |
| Driver | وعم تحكي عربي؟! | w 3am te7ké 3arabé?! | Und du sprichst Arabisch?! | And you speak Arabic?! |
| Daniel | شوي شوي. بحكي ألماني وإنكليزي، وهلّق عم بتعلم لبناني. | shwayy shwayy. be7ké almané w inglizé, w halla2 3am bet3allam lebnéné. | Langsam langsam. Ich spreche Deutsch und Englisch, und jetzt lerne ich Libanesisch. | Little by little. I speak German and English, and now I'm learning Lebanese. |
| Driver | برافو عليك! | bravo 3lék! | Bravo! | Good for you! |

## Grammar point: nisba (origin adjectives)
Country → person: add **-é** (m) / **-iyyé** (f):
almanya → almané/almaniyyé · lebnén → lebnéné/lebnéniyyé ·
faransa → fransawé · suriyya → suré

## Drills
1. **Nisba machine:** teacher: country → student: m + f adjective. 10 countries.
2. **Interview chain:** min wén inta? → shu 3am ta3mel? → shu bte7ké? — answered then re-asked, 5 cycles, speeding up.
3. **Truth round:** student answers all questions truthfully about himself, no notes.

## Role-play variations
- Taxi interrogation (dialogue).
- Party: friend-of-a-friend asks the same questions — casual register, add kamén ("inta kamén min Berlin?").
- Complication: driver assumes you're French — correct him politely: la2, ana mish fransawé, ana almané.
