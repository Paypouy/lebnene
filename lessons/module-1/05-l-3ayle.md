# Lesson 05 — Family & people

**Scenario:** Show a photo, name everyone in it, answer "are you married?"
without panic.

## Warm-up
Full taxi-driver interview (L04) at speed, both directions.

## Dialogue

> Colleague Rana sees Daniel's phone wallpaper.

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rana | مين هيدي؟ | min haidé? | Wer ist das (f)? | Who's that (f)? |
| Daniel | هيدي أختي، وهيدا بيّي. | haidé ekhté, w haida bayyé. | Das ist meine Schwester, und das ist mein Vater. | That's my sister, and that's my dad. |
| Rana | وين ساكنين؟ | wén séknin? | Wo wohnen sie? | Where do they live? |
| Daniel | بألمانيا. إمي وبيّي بميونيخ. | b-almanya. immé w bayyé b-München. | In Deutschland. Meine Mutter und mein Vater in München. | In Germany. My mom and dad in Munich. |
| Rana | إنت متجوّز؟ | inta mejjawwaz? | Bist du verheiratet? | Are you married? |
| Daniel | لأ، بعدني عازب. | la2, ba3dné 3ézeb. | Nein, ich bin noch ledig. | No, I'm still single. |
| Rana | عندك ولاد؟ | 3andak wléd? | Hast du Kinder? | Do you have kids? |
| Daniel | لأ! بس عندي كلب. | la2! bass 3andé kaleb. | Nein! Aber ich habe einen Hund. | No! But I have a dog. |

## Grammar point: possessive suffixes (the workhorse of Arabic)

| my | your (m) | your (f) | his | her |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| -é | -ak | -ik | -o | -a |
| bét**é** | bét**ak** | bét**ik** | bét**o** | bét**a** |

Irregulars to memorize as words: immé/bayyé (my mum/dad), akhé (my brother),
ekhté (my sister). **3and- = to have:** 3andé, 3andak, 3anda…

## Drills
1. **Suffix ladder:** teacher: bét → student: bété, bétak, bétik, béto, béta. Then: telefon, sheghel, rfi2.
2. **Photo round:** student shows 3 real photos: min haida? answered in full sentences.
3. **3and- blitz:** 3andak siyyara? 3andik kaleb? — yes/no answers with é/la2 + full form.

## Role-play variations
- Wallpaper conversation (dialogue).
- Asking about THEIR family: w inté? 3andik wléd? kam akh 3andik?
- Complication: nosy relative mode — "lésh ba3dak 3ézeb?!" (why are you still single?!) — deflect with humor: ba3dné zghir! (I'm still young!)
