# Lesson 02 — Sounds & the politeness toolkit

**Scenario:** You sound noticeably less foreign, and you can thank, excuse
yourself, and respond to courtesies the Lebanese way.

## Warm-up (5 min)
Full lesson-01 greeting exchange at natural speed, both genders.

## Part A — The sounds German doesn't have

| Sound | Anchor | Minimal pairs to drill |
|---|---|---|
| ع (3) | tight throat, voiced — NOT a vowel | 3am (uncle/doing) vs. am; sa3a (hour) vs. saa |
| ح (7) | breathy h, like fogging a mirror hard | 7ar (hot) vs. har; mni7 |
| خ (kh) | German **ch in „Bach"** — you have this! | khalas, khebez |
| غ (gh) | German **r in „Rede"** (uvular) — you have this too | ghali, shoghol |
| ق → 2 | glottal stop, like German „be**'**achten" | 2alb, 2ahwé, ba2a |
| imala é | long e like „**See**", where MSA has a/é | bét (house), lebnéné, ktéb |

> Teaching note: lean on the German anchors — kh and gh are free wins;
> spend the time on ع and ح.

## Dialogue (politeness in action)

> Daniel drops his pen; a colleague picks it up.

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |

## Drills
1. **Minimal-pair gauntlet:** teacher says 3am/am, 7ar/har randomly; student raises left/right hand. Then roles reverse — student produces, teacher judges.
2. **Politeness ping-pong:** teacher throws the situation (hands you something / you sneeze / someone eats), student fires the formula + correct reply.
3. **2-words:** read aloud: 2alb, 2ahwé, ba2a, da2i2a, wara2 — glottal stop every time.

## Role-play variations
- You arrive late: soré + ma3lésh exchange.
- Someone serves you coffee: mérsi/yeslamo → alla yeslamak → sa7tén.
- Ask for the bathroom in a restaurant, get directions, thank.
