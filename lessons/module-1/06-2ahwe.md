# Lesson 06 — At the café

**Scenario:** You take a Lebanese friend out for coffee and run the entire
interaction yourself.

## Warm-up
Family questions (L05) rapid-fire; suffix ladder on two new nouns.

## Dialogue

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Waiter | أهلا! شو بتحبّوا تشربوا؟ | ahla! shu bet7ebbo teshrabo? | Hallo! Was möchtet ihr trinken? | Hi! What would you like to drink? |
| Daniel | بدّي قهوة، وإنتي ليلى؟ | baddé 2ahwé, w inté Layla? | Ich nehme einen Kaffee, und du, Layla? | I'll have a coffee — Layla? |
| Layla | شاي، بليز. | shéy, bliz. | Tee, bitte. | Tea, please. |
| Waiter | القهوة وسط ولّا حلوة؟ | l-2ahwé wasat walla 7elwé? | Der Kaffee mittel oder süß? | Coffee medium or sweet? |
| Daniel | وسط. وعطينا قنينة مي كمان. | wasat. w 3atina 2anninet mayy kamén. | Mittel. Und gib uns auch eine Flasche Wasser. | Medium. And give us a bottle of water too. |
| Waiter | تكرم عيونكن. | tekram 3yunkon. | Sehr gerne. | With pleasure. |
| *(später)* | | | | |
| Daniel | الحساب، إذا بتريد. | l7séb, iza betrid. | Die Rechnung, bitte. | The bill, please. |
| Waiter | عشرين دولار. | 3eshrin dolar. | Zwanzig Dollar. | Twenty dollars. |
| Daniel | تفضّل. الباقي إلك. | tfaddal. lbé2é ilak. | Bitte. Der Rest ist für dich. | Here. Keep the change. |

## Grammar point: badd- (want) — conjugates like a noun with suffixes
baddé · baddak · baddik · baddo · badda · badna · badkon · baddon
Negation: **ma baddé** (no -sh in Lebanese).

## Drills
1. **badd- ladder:** all 8 forms on 2ahwé, then on shéy, then ma badd-.
2. **Order relay:** teacher plays waiter with curveballs (ma fi 3asir — we're out of juice) → student adapts: tayyeb, jibelné mayy.
3. **Walla-questions:** 2ahwé walla shéy? 7elo walla morr? hon walla take-away? — instant answers.

## Role-play variations
- Full café scene including paying.
- Ordering for 3 people (badda... baddo... w ana baddé...).
- Complication: bill seems wrong — la7za, l7séb ghalat? (intro for L23 complaints).
