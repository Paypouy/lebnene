# Lesson 19 — Future plans

## Grammar point
**ra7 + verb WITHOUT b-:** beshteghel (I work) → **ra7 eshteghel** (I'll work).
Soft future/intention: **baddé** + verb. And the famous **inshalla** —
sincerity ranges from "definitely" to "never"; tone is everything.

## Dialogue

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sami | شو رح تعمل بالويكند؟ | shu ra7 ta3mel bil-weekend? | Was machst du am Wochenende? | What are you doing this weekend? |
| Daniel | السبت رح روح عالبحر، والأحد بدّي ريّح. | l-sabet ra7 rou7 3al-ba7er, w l-a7ad baddé rayye7. | Samstag fahre ich ans Meer, Sonntag will ich entspannen. | Saturday the beach, Sunday I want to rest. |
| Sami | تعا معنا الأحد! رح نعمل برَبكيو. | ta3a ma3na l-a7ad! ra7 na3mel barbecue. | Komm Sonntag mit! Wir machen ein Barbecue. | Come with us Sunday! We're doing a barbecue. |
| Daniel | والله؟ تمام، رح جي. شو جيب معي؟ | walla? tamém, ra7 jé. shu jib ma3é? | Echt? Okay, ich komme. Was soll ich mitbringen? | Really? Okay, I'll come. What should I bring? |
| Sami | ولا شي، بس حالك! | wala shé, bass 7élak! | Gar nichts, nur dich selbst! | Nothing, just yourself! |
| Daniel | لأ لأ، رح جيب حلو. | la2 la2, ra7 jib 7elo. | Nein, nein, ich bringe was Süßes mit. | No no, I'll bring dessert. |

> Culture: "bass 7élak" is ritual. You ALWAYS bring something anyway —
> sweets, fruit, or wine depending on the host.

## Drills
1. Present→future flip: 10 verbs, drop the b-.
2. Calendar walk: bukra…, ba3ed bukra…, l-sabet…, l-jem3a l-jéyé… one plan each.
3. Certainty ladder: same plan said with akid / inshalla / yemkén — discuss what each signals.

## Role-play variations
- Barbecue invitation (dialogue), then YOU invite the teacher somewhere.
- Postpone politely: ma fiyyé bukra, mn2addem 3a ba3ed bukra? (can't tomorrow — move it?)
