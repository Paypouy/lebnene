# Lesson 20 — On the phone

> The phone is the hardest A2 skill: no lips to read. From this lesson on,
> ALL roleplays in this lesson happen back-to-back (not facing) or on an
> actual call.

## Dialogue

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Secretary | ألو، عيادة الدكتور خوري. | alo, 3iyédet l-doktor Khoury. | Hallo, Praxis Dr. Khoury. | Hello, Dr. Khoury's clinic. |
| Daniel | مرحبا، بدّي إحجز موعد، إذا بتريدي. | mar7aba, baddé e7joz maw3ad, iza betridé. | Hallo, ich möchte bitte einen Termin buchen. | Hi, I'd like to book an appointment please. |
| Secretary | تكرم. بكرا الساعة عشرة منيح؟ | tekram. bukra l-sé3a 3ashra mni7? | Gerne. Morgen um zehn, passt das? | Sure. Tomorrow at ten OK? |

## Drills
1. Spelling your name with Arabic letter names — drilled until smooth.
2. Reschedule chain: every confirmed slot gets a problem → mn2addem/mn2akhkher.
3. Bad-line simulation: teacher mumbles/cuts out → shu? ma sme3tak, 3id iza betrid (repeat please).

## Role-play variations
- Doctor booking (dialogue), back-to-back.
- Call a restaurant: table for 4, Friday 8pm, one vegetarian.
- Real call homework prep: script then improvise.
