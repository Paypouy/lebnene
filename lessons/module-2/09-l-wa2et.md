# Lesson 09 — Time & days

## Dialogue

| | Arabic | Translit | Deutsch | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rana | قدّي الساعة؟ | 2addé l-sé3a? | Wie spät ist es? | What time is it? |
| Daniel | الساعة تلاتة إلا ربع. | l-sé3a tlété illa rbe3. | Viertel vor drei. | Quarter to three. |
| Rana | الميتينغ الساعة تلاتة! | l-meeting l-sé3a tlété! | Das Meeting ist um drei! | The meeting's at three! |
| Daniel | معليش، في وقت. كل تنين نفس الشي. | ma3lésh, fi wa2et. kell tanén nafs l-shé. | Kein Stress, ist noch Zeit. Jeden Montag dasselbe. | Relax, there's time. Same thing every Monday. |

Time formula: **l-sé3a + number (+ w noss / w rbe3 / illa rbe3)**.
German parallel: „halb" logic differs — *tnén w noss* = **2:30** (zwei und
halb, NOT halb drei!). Flag this hard for German speakers.

## Drills
1. Clock blitz: teacher shows times on phone, student says them; reverse.
2. Day math: "lyom tanén — shu bukra? shu mbéri7?"
3. Schedule grid: when is X? — l-meeting émta? l-sé3a arb3a w noss.

## Role-play variations
- Running late for a meeting (dialogue) + apologizing (L02 formulas).
- Agreeing a time to meet a friend: bukra l-sé3a khamsé? la2, ba3dén shwayy…

## Assignment
- Anki deck 09. Say the time out loud (Arabic) every time you check your watch this week — seriously, every time.
- Voice note: your weekly schedule, day by day, with times.
